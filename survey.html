Invitation to survey

Tension Prediction in Non-Tonal Music: A Comparative Study

The purpose of this project is to identify and quantify aspects of non-tonal music that cause the state of the art musical tension prediction algorithms to deliver suboptimal results. Tension, or musical tension in the sense here, implies the feeling that something important is about to happen in the music.

You will be asked to continuously rate the tension you perceive while a musical repertoire is presented to you. To do this, you will enter the Pavlovia server running the experiment software and follow the steps as they are presented to you. The steps of the experiment are as follows:

  1. You will adjust your headphone volume in accordance with the software.
  2. You will be presented with the digital slider and the music, where each piece will be listened to for a minute. You will be asked to move the digital slider one way to no tension, implying a decrease in tension, or the other way, implying an increase in tension. This will continue until the repertoire is presented to you in full in random order, taking roughly 25 minutes together with the pauses between pieces.

To participate, simply respond to a.kuru@sussex.ac.uk with the signed consent form (linked below) and you’ll be sent the link to the browser based survey.
