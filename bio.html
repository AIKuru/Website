Bio

Between the lab and the studio.

A researcher and composer working where music psychology, electroacoustic practice, and live coding meet.

Short

I’m a researcher at the University of Sussex studying how electronic and drone music create the sensation of tension — and a composer and performer putting that work into sound. My research pairs listener studies with acoustic-feature analysis; my music runs from fixed-media electroacoustic pieces to techno-industrial productions and modular hardware.

Long

My practice is built around a single question: what makes sound feel like it is pulling toward something, or letting go? On the research side I model psychoacoustic tension from measurable features — loudness, spectral centroid, roughness, onset density, inharmonicity — and test those models against how people actually rate what they hear.

That framework is grounded in the musique concrète and spectromorphology traditions — Schaeffer, Chion, Smalley — and it feeds directly back into composition. The studio work spans granular and Eurorack modular synthesis, neural audio, and a techno-industrial aesthetic built from industrial and combustion source material.

I also work on live coding as an embodied, expressive practice — treating typing itself as a musical gesture rather than a neutral means of entering instructions.
