Research

Recent research projects.

Work spanning live-coding gesture, audio games, video game music, and spectral composition — the recurring thread is musical tension and the expressive potential of computer music systems.

Typing as a Musical Gesture in Live-Coding

2024 · MA final project, University of Sussex

An investigation of typing in live-coding from both theoretical and practical perspectives — treating typing as musical gesture, acoustic phenomenon, and an accidental by-product of interaction with the keyboard. Realised through a velocity-sensitive mechanical keyboard, microphones, and a SuperCollider project that feeds its sound-generation algorithms from keyboard and audio input. The work proposes “expressive typing”: consciously typing in a musical manner, tested and critiqued through a dedicated performance.

Areas: live coding, typing, keyboard, augmented instruments, computer music.

Prescience — experimental audio game

2024 · Sonic Media module, University of Sussex

An audio-only shooter set in a science-fiction world that uses sound spatialisation as its principal gameplay element. Players build a mental image of the space from triggered sounds and navigate accordingly. The sound design balances gameplay and aesthetic function through a modular vertical arrangement technique built on temporal changes in musical tension — composing material that creates dissonance when heard simultaneously and mapping it to the most engaging elements.

Increasing Interactivity in Video Game Music

2023 · MA thesis & published paper, Istanbul Technical University

A study of dynamic music practice in games, proposing an adaptive model inspired by the narrative principle of tension — suspense and repose. Game data and events are mapped to musical parameters (dissonance in melodic voices, amplitude in backing voices, tempo in rhythmic voices) in a way that supports dramatically conflicting structures, then implemented and evaluated for a real-time strategy game.

Keywords: game music, interactive/adaptive music, mapping, nonlinear composition.

Bells

2024 · Composition project, University of Sussex

A spectralist composition that explores the inner characteristics of a bell sound — its partials, inharmonic ratios, and noise — through analysis and interpretation. Harmonic qualities of the source feed algorithmic functions to generate material, and the temporal changes of the sound are superimposed onto the larger structure, balancing algorithmic process with an intuitive, improvisatory approach.

Keywords: computer music, additive synthesis, harmonic series, inharmonicity, intonation.

The End

2024 · Music & Media of Performance final project, University of Sussex

An experimental multimedia work combining audio, video, and theatrical performance, mixing channels of communication through the performer’s gestures. Thematically eschatological, its harsh audiovisual textures are heightened by noise generated from household items on stage via an EMF receiver.
