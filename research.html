Research

Modelling tension in sound.

Psychoacoustic modelling, live-coding gesture, and neural audio — the throughline is how measurable features of sound shape what a listener feels.

Active projects

2024–

TenseMusic — psychoacoustic tension modelling

A feature-driven model of perceived tension for electronic and drone music, extended from Barchet et al. (2024). Nine acoustic features extracted in NumPy/SciPy, feeding an LSTM tension-rating study with a purpose-built stimulus corpus and multi-participant listener data.

Model · Study
2024–

Typing as a musical gesture in live coding

A practice-based account of expressive typing as a learnable sensorimotor craft — re-embodying the live coder while straining live coding’s ideals of liveness and transparency. Built around a Hall-effect keyboard with velocity sensing and a SuperCollider mapping.

Paper · Practice
2023–

Neural audio & interactive sound installation

RAVE-based timbral systems and the landscape of neural-audio work across SEAMUS / NIME / AIMC — including corpus design for physical, textured, and techno-industrial source material.

Neural audio
2023–

Microsound instruments & the tension pipeline

VST concepts grounded in Roads’ Microsound — a grainlet parameter-coupler with OSC tension integration, connecting granular synthesis to the modelling work above.
